About the Platform

The Hub for DLSUD's
Gaming Community

GamersHub is a university-focused esports management and social engagement platform built for De La Salle University Dasmariñas — bringing verified matchmaking, official tournaments, livestream discovery, and student interaction into one unified space.

The Problem

Esports at DLSUD Was Fragmented

The university's growing gaming community was spread across disconnected tools — chat apps, spreadsheets, and unofficial group chats — making it difficult to coordinate, verify participants, or build a real sense of campus identity around esports.

📡

No Central Platform

Tournament brackets, stream links, and team registrations lived in separate places with no single authoritative source for students or organizers.

🔒

No Identity Verification

Without school-level verification, organizers had no way to ensure participants were genuine DLSUD students, undermining the integrity of university competitions.

📊

No Operational Visibility

Administrators lacked analytics, audit trails, and reporting tools — making it hard to monitor platform activity, manage content, or demonstrate accountability.

What GamersHub Does

Everything a University Esports
Community Needs

GamersHub was designed around the actual workflows of student gamers, university organizers, and platform administrators — not generic social media patterns.

🔐

Secure Authentication

Multi-factor authentication with email verification, Google OAuth integration, and school-based identity verification to keep the community trusted.

🏆

Tournament Management

Browse official DLSUD tournaments, view brackets, follow match schedules, and track standings — all published and managed by verified administrators.

📺

Livestream Discovery

Find and watch live and recorded streams tied to campus gaming events. Admins can publish, moderate, and manage stream visibility in real time.

💬

Community Feed

A social feed scoped to your network — posts, reactions, and comments from your accepted connections, keeping the experience relevant and personal.

📈

Analytics & Reports

Platform-wide dashboards for administrators: user registration trends, engagement metrics, top games, and exportable CSV reports for stakeholders.

🗂️

Audit Trail

Every administrative action — role changes, stream moderation, content removals — is logged with actor, timestamp, and context for full accountability.

Who It's For

Three Roles, One Platform

GamersHub serves different members of the DLSUD gaming community with purpose-built access levels — each with a tailored dashboard and a distinct set of capabilities.

🎮 Player

Student Gamer

Any verified DLSUD student. The default experience — focused on community, competition browsing, and social interaction.

  • Follow friends and view their posts
  • React, comment, and share on the feed
  • Browse tournaments and livestreams
  • View personal leaderboard standing
  • Manage profile and account settings
🛡️ Admin

Platform Moderator

Assigned by a superadmin. Responsible for day-to-day content operations, moderation, and event management.

  • Publish and moderate streams
  • Create and manage events
  • View platform analytics
  • Export user and activity reports
  • Review audit logs
👑 Superadmin

System Governance

Highest privilege level. Oversees the entire platform, manages role assignments, and maintains systemic integrity.

  • Promote or demote user roles
  • Full access to all admin capabilities
  • System-wide oversight dashboard
  • Manage admin accounts
  • Platform-level governance controls

Scope & Technology

What's Included and How It's Built

GamersHub was developed as a thesis project for De La Salle University Dasmariñas, covering a defined scope of esports platform features backed by a microservices-inspired architecture.

Platform Scope

  • Authentication with MFA and Google OAuth
  • School-based identity verification for DLSUD students
  • Role-based access control across three privilege levels
  • Social feed with friend-scoped post visibility
  • Reactions and comment interactions
  • Tournament browsing and schedule viewing
  • Livestream discovery and playback
  • Event calendar managed by administrators
  • Leaderboard rankings per game
  • Admin analytics, CSV reports, and audit logging

Technology Stack

Node.js Express SQL Server HTML / CSS / JS Google OAuth 2.0 Nodemailer bcrypt Multer Bootstrap 5 REST API Microservices Architecture Vercel

Limitations

  • Limited to DLSUD students and institutional affiliates
  • Livestreaming relies on external playback URLs (not self-hosted)
  • No native mobile application at this time
  • Real-time features use polling rather than WebSockets
